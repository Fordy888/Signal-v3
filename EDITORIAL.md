# DTL Signal — Editorial Principles

These are constitutional principles, not implementation details.

Code will change. Models will change. Prompts will change. Sources will change.

The editorial philosophy should remain remarkably stable.

---

Signal is an executive decision-support system.

Its purpose is not to report more news, aggregate more sources, or generate more content. Its purpose is to improve executive judgment by identifying the developments, patterns, and strategic implications that matter most.

Every editorial, technical, and product decision should be evaluated against this purpose.

---

## 1. Protect the Editorial Identity

Signal is not a news aggregator. It is an executive decision-support product.

Every enhancement must answer one question: *"Does this improve executive judgment?"*

If not, it does not belong.

---

## 2. Section 8 Is the Product

The earlier sections create awareness. Section 8 creates value.

Its role is to connect unrelated developments into a coherent strategic insight.

Not summarise. Interpret.

---

## 3. Ruthlessly Protect Quality

14 exceptional insights are better than 24 average ones.

Signal earns trust through judgment, not volume.

The scoring threshold should remain conservative.

---

## 4. Pattern Recognition Over Headlines

The most valuable output is not identifying the biggest story. It is identifying the biggest pattern.

Readers should finish each edition feeling they understand what is changing, not simply what happened.

---

## 5. Think Like an Editor

Every feature should strengthen the editorial experience.

Less clutter. More clarity. More interpretation. More confidence.

---

## 6. Reliability Is a Competitive Advantage

Executives build trust in products that arrive every morning without fail.

Consistency is part of the product.

---

## 7. The Filter

Before adding, changing, or removing anything from Signal, ask:

> "Does this improve executive judgment?"

If the answer is no, do not ship it.

---

## 8. Respect the Reader's Time

Signal should always reduce the time required for an executive to understand what matters.

If an edition takes longer to read without increasing insight, we have failed.

Our objective is not comprehensive coverage. Our objective is compressed understanding.

Everything should move the reader from information to understanding to action as efficiently as possible.

---

*Established 28 June 2026. These principles govern all editorial, technical, and product decisions for DTL Signal.*
